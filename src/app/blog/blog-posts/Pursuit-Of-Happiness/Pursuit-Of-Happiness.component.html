Early Years

I honestly don't know where to begin, but I think I will give some more background about myself. I am a recent graduate from Clarkson University where I got my Undergraduate degree in Software Engineering with a Minor in Mathematics. I have been using tech and working with computers virtually my whole life. My first real excitment and exposure for and with tech, was around the age of 8 or 9 when I started First Lego League Robotics. I had an amazing experience with this program, I had amazing coaches and mentors as well as teammates and friends. Some of my fondest childhood memories are from this time. I would not be the person I am today with those core STEM experiences.

I continued with robotics for about 5 years and stopped when I got to highschool. While also competing in Robotics I played a variety of sports like basketball, baseball, and cross-country. I wasn't very good at anything besides running so I continued doing other extracurriculers like CyberPatriot and taking my first real stabs at coding. I built my first PC and really fell in love with Computer Hardware, which is a passion of mine outside of my career.

I then went to college and first declared as an Engineering and Management Undergraduate at Clarkson University.

The Making of Me

Looking back at that time and that decision, I have some regret. One of the driving factors in the decision was my low self-esteem. I did not believe in my ability to complete upper level Math and Science courses but to be honest I didn't even give myself a chance. Due to some poor choices and outlooks on my position at the time I performed very poorly my first year of college. Becoming Academically Seperated and having to decide what I was going to do with my Life or what I would do after having this idea of what it would look like since I started Robotics at 8, was hard to reconcile with.

I was the first class to go to College with Covid in 2020 so that probably didn't help but due to this Clarkson gave me a second chance. I decided to go with general engineering until I could make decision on my Major. I started to talk to professors as well as students and was heavily considering Computer and Software Engineering. With the lucrative field of software engineering in the 2020's it seemed like a risk but one I was willing to take. I took a few summer courses that introduced me to C++ and some basic programming concepts but I knew from then on where I was gonna be going.

Since then I have made some amazing strides to really pursue a life of work I will enjoy and inspire others with. This decision has left me feeling like I'm a kid doing Robotics again. I have become obsessed with Web Development since I first started working on it. I was fortunate enough to secure an internship as a Web Developer at Navy Federal going into my final year of college. I graduated in May of 2025 and have been working as a fulltime Software Developer at Navy Federal ever since.
